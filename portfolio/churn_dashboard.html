Key Finding: Month-to-Month Contracts + New Customers = Highest Risk

The model identifies contract type and tenure as the two most actionable churn levers. Customers on month-to-month contracts who are less than 12 months old churn at 47.7% — nearly 17× the rate of two-year contract holders (2.8%). A targeted retention offer (contract upgrade incentive, loyalty discount) aimed at this segment could prevent the majority of the $334K+ revenue at risk identified in the top-100 customer list.
