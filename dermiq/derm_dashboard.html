DermIQ — Clinic Intelligence Platform
The analytical layer that sits between a dermatology practice's EHR system and its business decisions. Built on real CMS Medicare data benchmarks with synthetic patient-level records to demonstrate what a $300/month SaaS product would deliver to a solo dermatologist in Texas.

Interview Talking Points
"I anchored the synthetic clinic data to real CMS Medicare benchmarks so the economics are defensible. A solo TX dermatologist sees ~90 patients/week at $106 average Medicare payment — that's the baseline everything is calibrated to."
Layer 0–1: CMS data download → benchmark extraction → calibrated synthetic generation. The fake clinic matches real national averages.
"Medicare data shows the medical side — I modeled the cosmetic revenue gap separately since it's entirely cash-pay and not captured by CMS. Cosmetic is ~38% of a typical derm practice but invisible to any Medicare-based benchmarking tool."
Layer 1: CMS cosmetic gap analysis. This is the core insight a real SaaS product would sell against.
"The no-show model quantifies $216K in annual revenue at risk for a typical solo practice. Targeting the top 20% highest-risk appointments with double reminders could recover $43K+ annually — that's the number that makes a clinic owner pay attention."
Layer 4: HistGradientBoosting no-show classifier with permutation importance and business impact quantification.
"LTV:CAC analysis shows doctor referrals produce the best lifetime value per dollar spent — far exceeding Instagram despite lower volume. That's a concrete budget reallocation recommendation a marketing analyst would make."
Layer 5: Channel attribution with ROAS, LTV:CAC ratio, 2-year retention comparison, and actionable recommendation.
"This is the analytical layer that sits between a doctor's EHR system and their business decisions. Most solo practices have Athena or Epic for clinical records, nothing for analytics. DermIQ is that gap."
Product positioning: sub-10 physician practices are underserved by enterprise analytics tools. Target customer = dermatologist with 1-2 locations, $800K–$2M revenue, no dedicated analytics staff.
